About me

I am a PhD researcher at the University of the Highlands and Islands, affiliated with the University of Strathclyde, working at the intersection of zooarchaeology, ancient DNA analysis, and stable isotope studies. My research is funded by the Scottish Graduate School for Arts and Humanities.

My work is grounded in Human-Animal Studies, Actor-Network Theory, and posthumanist theory. I am interested in moving beyond frameworks that treat animals purely as economic resources, and instead in recovering the entangled lives, relationships, and agencies that shaped human and animal communities together.

Before beginning my PhD, I completed an MRes exploring Scottish Iron Age faunal assemblages. I am based in Scotland.

Research

My project examines four Iron Age sites across Orkney, drawing on biomolecular and zooarchaeological methods to reconstruct the social and ecological relationships between humans and four key species.

Site 01

Pool

A multi-period settlement site spanning the Neolithic to Norse periods, with exceptional preservation across a long sequence of occupation.

Site 02

Howe

An important broch site yielding evidence of complex Iron Age occupation and a rich assemblage of faunal material.

Site 03

Mine Howe

A ritual site distinguished by its unusual underground shaft structure, offering a distinct context for examining human-animal deposits.

Site 04

The Cairns

An ongoing excavation revealing a substantial Iron Age settlement with significant zooarchaeological assemblages currently under study.
